---------------------------------------
SCORING MODEL (Total = 100)
---------------------------------------

Payment Safety (20 points)
20 → PayPal available
10 → Visa/Mastercard + Apple Pay/Google Pay (no PayPal)
5 → Cards only
0 → Bank transfer / crypto only

Shipping Transparency & Reliability (15 points)
15 → Clear origin, delivery timeframes, tracking clarity
8 → Some detail but vague or incomplete
0 → Unclear, missing, or misleading

Returns Process (20 points)
20 → Easy returns, prepaid label, local return path
10 → Returns allowed but with friction
0 → No returns or highly restrictive

Return Costs & Fairness (10 points)
10 → Free returns + full refund
5 → Fee or deduction applied
0 → Expensive, unclear, or store-credit heavy

Overseas Risk (10 points)
10 → Local fulfilment + local returns
5 → Mixed fulfilment model
0 → Overseas fulfilment and/or overseas returns

Policy Clarity & Transparency (10 points)
10 → Clear and detailed policies
5 → Some gaps or ambiguity
0 → Missing or confusing

Customer Experience Signals (10 points)
10 → Consistently positive reputation
5 → Mixed signals
0 → Consistently negative

Website & Business Credibility (5 points)
5 → Professional presence, business identity evident
3 → Minor concerns
0 → Suspicious or incomplete identity

---------------------------------------
EVIDENCE INTERPRETATION RULES
---------------------------------------

Structured signals present in the EVIDENCE section MAY be treated as valid verification. These include:

• Payment logos or identifiers found in HTML or scripts
• Platform indicators (e.g. Stripe, Shopify payments)
• Policy content available via linkable pages (e.g. /refunds, /returns, /shipping)
• Shipping origin disclosures
• Delivery timeframe statements
• Refund eligibility or return conditions
• Contact or legal identity signals (e.g. ABN, address)
• Checkout/payment integration references

Such indicators ARE considered equivalent to UI-level verification even where checkout access was not retrieved.

Only treat a signal as "Not verifiable" where:
• It is implied but not explicitly stated
• Conflicting signals are present
• It appears only in user-generated content

---------------------------------------
OVERSEAS FULFILMENT DETECTION
---------------------------------------

Text in policy or footer content indicating:

• “Ships from overseas”
• “International warehouse”
• “Shipping from China”
• “Fulfilled by partner”
• “Delivery 14–30 business days”

SHOULD be treated as verified fulfilment origin.

If overseas fulfilment or overseas return address is explicitly mentioned:
→ Overseas Risk = 0
